## ENGLISH

### Approved 9 July 2026

- **Battery-wide report laws.** No numbers rendered actor-side, ever — bars only; no band names, ranks, or flags shown; condition reads are prose-and-bars. Bands: UPPER mean ≥ 3.75 · MIDDLE 2.75–3.74 · LOWER < 2.75 · HEDGE ±0.15 (hedged openers replace a block's first sentence). All values computed and stored append-only for coach render and research. Micro-reveal after every module; felt-experience check-in after first full view of any report; standing coaching offer, unconditional; coach renders are views of the same data and ship after actor reports.

- **Module 3 delivers no standalone actor report** (the no-mirror decision). The M3 Report Content Pack v0.1 was approved and superseded the same day; it is retained as the source for the M3 coach render and the Emotion Exploration tool. **Strengths-only law (hard):** no Module 3 result ever renders actor-side except affirmatively — the most open colours, named only in the Core Report's entrance headers. The role-hangover flag has **no actor-visible consequence**; it is computed, stored, and surfaced coach-side only.

- **The Core Report** (name chosen by Filiz; actor-facing title "Your Core Report") is the prize at core completion — a working instrument, not a profile. Structure per Filiz's edit: opening frame → the four entrances FIRST (top 2 APS domains + top 2 emotional systems; ties render together; D9 never an entrance) → Questions to Ask in Character Design (doorway-conditional) → Coming Back Cleanly (unconditional, with the Module 9 door) → Your Next Doors (APS-derived routing only) → close. No "how your set was built" chapter. The doorway report and APS report remain separately viewable.

- **The canonical boundary paragraph** (widened by Filiz): "the ACTIONS, the STORIES, the EMOTIONS or the THOUGHTS you build for a character are the character's — designed, authored, and owned by the character." This form propagates everywhere the boundary renders; the Turkish glossary inherits it.

- **Question register (law):** all written character questions are third person, leading with "your character" — never first person, never bare her/him. First person belongs to live coaching interviews only. The question sections were removed from the sixteen doorway reports and live only in the Core Report.

- **The construct rename:** "type" never appears actor-side. The construct is the actor's **doorway to the character design**; Module 1's display name is **The Doorway**; slug type_lens unchanged forever. The sixteen doorway names (The Spark … The Cornerstone) approved as a register; four-letter keys internal only, never rendered, including inside the doorway reports. This reverses the "type labels parked indefinitely" ruling. Untouchable always: Levesque's talent-pair names, 16personalities titles, Keirsey role-names.

- **The Bridge (ENFJ) drafted and approved at v0.2** — audit gate cleared by Filiz's approval; its Core Report question section approved with it. All sixteen doorways have approved reports. APS Actor Report Content Pack at v0.3 (rename sweep only; v0.2 content untouched).

- **Character Preparation Question Bank v0.1 scope:** DOORWAY blocks un-parked — they render as the Core Report's entrances. SAFEGUARD blocks remain parked actor-side, permanently.

- **Coach Core Report architecture (v0.1→v0.5):** one document per actor; intake portrait with aspirations verbatim as the contract; the sixteen doorway coaching blocks (Reaching them · Doors to the inner life · Enhancers) behind the boundary line ("we knock, we don't excavate"); the full palette coach-side; Section E.2 = the actor's question chapter verbatim + edge-selected safeguard blocks behind the safeguard frame ("asked one at a time they are craft; handed over as a list they become a diagnosis"); **the Coach Library** — the full bank, both voices, one standing copy in the coach workspace (decision c). Scope ruling: the bank is parked actor-side in full; coach-side it is fully visible. Module 9 stays outside this document.
